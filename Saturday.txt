Saturday is the seventh and last day of the week according to many commonly used calendars, but the second-to-last day according to ISO 86011. It is the day of the week following Friday and preceding Sunday12. Its name comes from the Latin dies Saturni, meaning "day of the planet Saturn"13, which was kept in the Germanic calendar unlike other days that were renamed after Germanic deities4. In some cultures, Saturday is also associated with bathing4. Saturday is often a day for sleeping in, being lazy, going out, or watching sports4. Examples of sentences using Saturday are: He will arrive next Saturday2. His birthday falls on a Saturday this year2. Saturday is the first official day of the regular two-day weekend4. The written abbreviation for Saturday is Sat.
